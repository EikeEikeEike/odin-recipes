Maple Mustard Salmon

How to make Maple Mustard Salmon

A quick roast at high heat is one of the breeziest ways to cook salmon. Stir together a tangy stone-ground mustard glaze and brush it over the fillets: You'll be done with prep before the oven finishes preheating.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ tablespoons stone-ground mustard
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice
  • ½ teaspoon minced garlic
  • ¼ teaspoon minced fresh thyme
  • 6 (6 ounce) frozen salmon fillets, thawed and patted dry
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Directions

Step 1

Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Line a large baking sheet with foil.

Step 2

Whisk together mustard, maple syrup, lemon juice, garlic, and thyme in a small bowl.

Step 3

Season salmon on both sides with salt and pepper and brush with oil. Arrange salmon on prepared baking sheet. Brush with mustard mixture.

Step 4

Bake in the preheated oven until salmon flakes easily with a fork, 8 to 10 minutes.

Cook's Note:

If your salmon has skin, arrange skin sides down on the baking sheet.
